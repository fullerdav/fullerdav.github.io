Why on earth are we going to the cloud?
Sierra is expensive, and the company is not what it used to be.

Sierra hasn't changed or developed in years but the world (and libraries) have changed a lot.
We used to be a building full of print books and journals.

We still have an important collection of print materials and other media but there is so much else that needs to be managed, especially our electronic resources.

It's complicated and Sierra isn't helping very much.
By moving to Alma we will be paying less for a system that helps us do our work better and has capabilities that we don't have now..

We will no longer need to pay for and maintain hardware, perform tape backups or sytem upgrades.
But why Alma?
Our only viable choices were Alma (Ex Libris) or WMS (OCLC)

EBSCO is developing a product called "Folio" which sounds appealing but still a long way from ready. There are open-source options which we ruled out mainly because of support.
We had vendor visits, made our own site visits and spoke to a sample of other sites running each system. We spoke to trusted contacts and looked at our peers (Oberlin Group libraries).
Among the Oberlin Group there is roughly an even divide between libraries that have chosen Alma or WMS to date. But the sample size is small and most of the Obie Group is mired with Sierra.

If you look at our expediture per FTE (which is relatively high) we are in a cohort of Obie libraries that have largely chosen Alma.
Expenditure per FTE is a measure of our commitment to undergraduate research. It was reassuring to see that we are in the top tier of Obie group libraries.

I also noticed that we are in the upper third in ILL lending, which is a sign of the quality of our collection.
One final observation about the marketplace, most of our peers that have chosen WMS made the choice before 2015, three years ago or more.

The recent trend seems to be toward Alma. Last year, nationwide, 80% of academic libriaries chose Alma.
What can we expect?
The "catalog" will be quite different. While our MARC records will still be in there they will be interfiled with other types of records representing different resources.

It will be called our "Institution Zone", at least that's what it wants to be called.

ERM is now URM, "Universal Resource Management."
The system is more integrated than anything we've ever had.

The discovery layer, "Summon" is the search interface sitting on top of our resources.

Lists of databases, even an A-Z list can be generated directly from our Institution Zone. Many things that have been scattered will be in one place.

Many ILL requests can be initiated directly by the patron.
The system will be able to exchange data more easily with other systems, including vendors, campus systems, and whatnot.
Alma includes analytic tools so that we can measure our collections and usage more easily to better understand what we do and communicate our value to the rest of the campus.
