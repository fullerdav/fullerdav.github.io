As we prepare tomove to a new library system it seems worthwhile to have a better understanding of our users, their needs and expectations of the library. Requests for items we don't own reflects an instance of need. ILL and ConnectNY are systems, among others, designed to meet those needs. Understanding the volume of requests in each system, by whom (faculty or student), when, in what subject areas, and the "turn around tme" for filling those requests are the goals for this analysis.

ConnectNY vs ILL

ConnectNY (6428) has been used more than ILL (2082) over this 4 year period. Students (4310/969) more than Faculty (2118/1113).

There are more than 10x as many students as faculty.

We typically say that ConnectNY takes about a week. That is roughly the median turnaround for ILL. So while ILL may take longer at least half of the requests are filled with the same timeliness. Within NY State the median turn around time for ILL is only 5 days which compares favorably to ConnectNY.

Turnaround varies more by library than any other factor. Average time to receive an item from SUNY Albany is 4.48 days, the New York State Library is 4.88, while Skidmore is 2.63. We receive items faster from Davidson College (4.62) in North Carolina than from Albany.

While we borrow items from all over the world international requests take longer and are probably outliers for this analysis.

IDS

We've been using IDS for about a year. IDS automates the more routine tasks, searching our catalog and remote catalogs to determine availability and routing requests more intelligently (than bare OCLC).

From January through September 2017 Union borrowed 573 returnables via Illiad, with 184 requests being filled within New York State and 136 of those were filled by IDS libraries. That is roughly 1/3 filled with NY and roughly 3/4 of those were by IDs.

IDS (returnables) had a slightly longer turn-around than non-IDS requests. That can be explained by Skidmore, a nearby non-IDS library that filled many requests. Also IDS speeds up the placing of requests but can't speed the delivery mechanisms.
