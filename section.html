Why on earth are we going to the cloud?

Sierra is expensive, and the company is not what it used to be.
Sierra hasn't changed or developed in years but the world (and libraries) have changed a lot.
We will no longer need to pay for and maintain hardware, perform tape backups or sytem upgrades.
By moving to Alma we will be paying less for a system that supports our work/goals/mission better.
We used to be a building full of print books and journals.
We still have an important collection of print materials but our virtual collections need to be managed.
It's a complicated and Sierra isn't helping very much.

But why Alma?

Our only viable choices were Alma (Ex Libris) or WMS (OCLC)
EBSCO is developing a product called "Folio" which sounds appealing but still a long way from ready.
There are open-source options which we ruled out mainly because of support.
Vendor visited, we made our own site visits and spoke to a sample of other sites running each system.
We spoke to trusted contacts and looked at our peers (Oberlin Group libraries).
Among the Oberlin Group there is roughly an even divide between libraries that have chosen Alma or WMS to date.
But the sample size is small and most of the Obie Group is still mired with Sierra.
Our expediture per FTE (which is relatively high) places us in a cohort of Obie Group libraries that have chosen Alma.
Expenditure per FTE is a measure of our commitment to undergraduate research.
It was reassuring that we are in the top tier of Obie group libraries.
I also noticed that we are in the upper third in ILL lending, which might be a sign of the quality of our collection.
Most of our peers that have chosen WMS made the choice before 2015, three years ago or more.
The recent trend seems to be toward Alma. Last year, nationwide, 80% of academic libriaries chose Alma.

What can we expect?

The system is more integrated than anything we've ever had.
Many things that have been scattered will be in one place.
The discovery layer, "Summon" is the search interface sitting on top of all our resources.
The "catalog" will be quite different. While our MARC records will still be in there they will be interfiled with other types of records.
It will be called our "Institution Zone", (at least that's what it wants to be called).
ERM is now URM, "Universal Resource Management."
Lists of databases, even an A-Z list can be generated directly from our Institution Zone.
The system will be able to exchange data more easily with other systems, vendors, campus systems, etc.
Many ILL requests can be initiated directly by the patron.
Analytic tools can measure our collections and usage to better understand what we do and communicate our value to the rest of the campus.
